Carousels

Sky.com uses many (many!) carousels which often take different approaches to implementation, and therefore they have varying levels of accessibility.

The homepage carousel currently provides the best accessibility experience, so this page talks about how to make that approach fully accessible. The homepage carousel does not currently provide a good experience for screen reader users as they have to navigate through all the hidden content.

Accessibility requirements

The primary requirements for carousels with content are that:

  • The controls are obvious, i.e. visible and clear about how they work.
  • Movement is either activated by the user, or can easily be paused by the user.
    (Controls for animated content)
  • Keyboard users (including screen readers):
    • Can tab to the carousel, starting with the controls.
    • Can tell which control has focus.
    • The carousel does not automatically move when in focus.
    • Tabbing (or moving through with a screen reader) does not get to hidden content.
  • Screen reader users can tell that it is a dynamic element (e.g. with a hidden heading at the start of the carousel).

The homepage carousel fulfils most of these requirements apart from preventing access to hidden content for screen reader users.

Methods

Currently a visual-keyboard user cannot tab to the hidden content, but screen reader users can. There are two possible ways to remove the hidden content for screen readers:

  1. Fully hide the hidden content, with display: none; or an equivalent.
  2. Dynamically add/remove the content from the tab index, applying tabindex="-1" to hidden content, and tabindex="0" to the visible content.

The second option may be easier for a carousel, as the non-visible content is actually in the background rather than hidden.

Examples

It was not possible in the time allotted for this toolkit to fully develop a working carousel, but one of the methods above should be straightforward to apply.

Testing

Similar to the requirements, in supported browsers and using a mouse, check that:

  • The controls are obvious, i.e. visible and clear about how they work.
  • Movement is either activated by the user, or can easily be paused by the user.

In supported browsers with a keyboard check that:

  • You can tab to the carousel, and get to the controls first.
  • You can see (via outline or other visible change) which control / link is highlighted.
  • The focus does not automatically change when in the carousel.
  • You cannot tab to hidden content.

Also, with common screen readers (JAWs, NVDA, VoiceOver/OSX & VoiceOver/iOS) check that:

  • You can tab to the carousel, and get to the controls first.
  • The focus does not automatically change when in the carousel.
  • You cannot tab to hidden content.
